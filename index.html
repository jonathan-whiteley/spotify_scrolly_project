How has the pandemic changed the world's taste in music?

Start scrolling to find out.

Throughout the ebbs and flows of the COVID-19 pandemic, music has remained a critical piece of how humans express, process, and cope with emotions.

Using Spotify’s Developer API and Spotify’s Global Top 200 Charts, we analyzed ways in which the pandemic has affected the types of music we consumed.

With March 2020 as the inflection point between pre and post-COVID (when the WHO officially declared a pandemic), we explored questions around shifts in genres, artists, songs, and listening trends.

1. Generally the most popular genres stayed popular...

With an overview of the genres that make up the daily Top 200 Charts, we see a pretty consistent breakdown over the last 4 years. Hip hop/rap and edm tracks consistently dominate.

However, by separating out each genre, we see upward trends in pop and latino music in early 2020, coinciding with periods of isolation for many people as stay at home orders were imposed across the world.

2. We listened to more pop and latino music...

In 2020 we sampled more new music. In fact, 294 more distinct songs charted in 2020 than 2019, largely driven by the increase in new pop and latino songs.

Hover over the chart to see if you recognize any of the individual songs across each genre and their associated chart date. 2021 data as of Sept 30, 2021.

With the pandemic driving many to relocate, we see an increased appetite for new music coincide with shifts in lifestyles throughout 2020.

3. We listened to more unique songs and artists...

This interactive tool allows you to explore how many different artists were featured on the Global Top 200 Charts.

During the pandemic, did we listen to the same artists? Using the drop down date selections to the right, explore how many artists were on the Global Top 200 Charts.

Did the number of songs artists were creating change? Select a specific artists’ name and analyze how many songs they had on the Top Charts over different time periods. Did your favorite artists release new music during the Pandemic?

4. We listened to happier songs, with less lyrics...

Overall, during the pandemic we listened to more valent tracks that contained less speechiness - but we can also examine seasonality impacts to our listening habits.

The live music peaks typically seen during the spring and summer months are no longer pronounced due to thousands of canceled concerts.

Due to state restrictions on nightlife and many canceled travel plans, there were less danceable songs listened to, particularly during late summer and early spring.

It also appears that the Pandemic brought on an early Christmas as seen in trends in accousticness and instrumentalness during the month of December.

In what seemed to be a time when everything in our lives changed drastically, music remained a constant.

However, it took on a new role for many of us and our tastes evolved as a result. This tool serves as a resource to anyone who shares our love of music and is keen to explore this evolution!
